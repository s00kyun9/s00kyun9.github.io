1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that caught my attention was the clean typography and well-organized layout. The harmony between font sizes, spacing, and alignment created a sense of balance that made the entire portfolio easy to read and visually appealing. The layout was flawless — everything felt perfectly placed without any distractions or clutter. This thoughtful design immediately drew me in and allowed me to focus on the content with ease. It gave a strong first impression of the artist’s refined aesthetic and made the experience feel immersive from the very beginning.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Clicked box 15 to view the artist’s information
  • Clicked the title to access the portfolio page
  • Clicked numbers below the image to explore sub-pages
  • Clicked the “W.” box to view additional work
  • Scrolled down the page
  • Moved stickers inside boxes to customize the layout

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time on the portfolio page. Each project was presented with minimal text and strong visuals, which encouraged exploration. The hover animations and smooth transitions between projects made it enjoyable to browse through multiple works and clearly conveyed the artist’s aesthetic.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most frequent action during the two-minute exploration was clicking on the numbered boxes to access different content. I especially enjoyed playing with the interactive stickers inside each box — they were fun to move around, so I kept trying them multiple times. This repeated behavior shows how the site’s intuitive structure and playful interaction naturally engage users.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The site is not just a gallery of works, but a portfolio designed to let users experience the artist’s visual language and philosophy. Through interaction and carefully curated visuals, it encourages engagement beyond passive viewing, ultimately leaving a strong impression of the artist’s identity and style.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Throughout the experience, the minimalist structure, fluid transitions, and original interaction elements subtly express the artist’s personality and creative direction. The calendar-style layout and the unique presentation of each box guide users to navigate the site like an exhibition space, allowing the content and design to communicate the purpose without explicit explanation.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The site appears to be designed for occasional visits rather than frequent use, especially when new work is added. Its project-focused layout and simple interface allow users to fully appreciate the content without needing to stay for long, emphasizing that the content itself is the core of the experience.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

With no features like login or bookmarks, the site encourages a passive and intuitive browsing style. Its static framework is designed to highlight the evolving content, positioning users more like viewers appreciating a curated collection rather than active participants.

9. What other media forms does the experience reference?

The overall structure seems to reference editorial design, independent publications, and digital art galleries. The grid-based calendar layout, generous spacing, and refined typography follow a visual language typical of design-focused media, adding a sense of professionalism and consistency throughout the site.

10. What does this reference communicate about how to act during your research experience?

The design encourages users to engage not just by flipping through content but by paying attention to visual composition and interaction. Each element’s placement and motion feel intentional, prompting users to observe and interpret, making their experience more active and involved.

11. What does this reference communicate about how to feel during your research experience?

The calm color palette, relaxed pacing, and restrained visuals guide users into a quiet and immersive state. With minimal distraction, the content becomes the focus, and the overall mood of the site encourages reflection and interpretation.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction and why?

Some interactions were not immediately intuitive, which made it slightly difficult to grasp the site’s structure at first. Especially without clear indicators of what each box contained, the initial exploration felt a bit disorienting.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction and why?

The smooth and responsive transitions between elements were especially satisfying. Even small interactions felt precise, and the overall flow and aesthetic direction perfectly matched the artist’s style, creating a highly refined user experience.
