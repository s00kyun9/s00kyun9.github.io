Final Reflection

At the beginning of this project, I focused heavily on visual aesthetics. However, as the process unfolded, I came to realize that emotional storytelling, immersive pacing, and the rhythm of sentiment were even more important. While I considered features like real-time preview or functional expansion, I ultimately chose to limit features and preserve the emotional flow.

Still, through this project, I identified some technical limitations and user experience gaps:

  • Users were not clearly informed about how to resize or rotate stickers, leading to confusion with the shortcut-based controls.
  • In some browser environments, flickering or residual traces appeared when stickers were moved.
  • The absence of a soft ‘undo’ function after submission resulted in users losing all content upon accidentally navigating away.
  • The 450-character limit sometimes made it difficult to fully express emotional messages.

Despite these challenges, I learned that even a simple web interaction can leave a strong impression when guided by emotional design and clear intent. Designing for emotional experiences — not just functionality — is a philosophy I will carry into future projects. In the next steps, I hope to address the usability concerns and improve the emotional clarity of the interaction even further.
